siPelakor is a UI/UX project aimed at addressing marine environmental issues through technology. Designed to support Sustainable Development Goal 14 (Life Below Water), the application uses gamification principles to encourage users to participate in coastal and marine cleanliness initiatives.

### From Wireframe to Final UI: The Dashboard

The foundation of the application's user experience was built on a thorough literature study regarding environmental engagement. I mapped out the core user journeys using low-fidelity wireframes to ensure intuitive navigation and structural integrity before applying the final aquatic-themed visual design.

### Core Functionality: Location Reporting

A critical feature of the platform is allowing users to accurately report environmental hazards. The design prioritized reducing friction during data entry, ensuring that users could easily pin GPS coordinates and submit reports without navigational confusion.

### Gamification & Reward Systems

To drive user retention and consistent engagement, the system architecture incorporates interactive elements that track progress and celebrate milestones. By tying in-app points earned from daily tasks to real-world rewards, the act of environmental stewardship becomes visually and tangibly rewarding.
